Why The Surface Room

Built in-studio. Chosen for the camera.

I

Made, not sourced

Every surface is designed and built in our Toronto studio. You're not renting the same flat-pack boards every other studio has — you're renting pieces with character that belong in editorial work.

II

Photographer-led

Each finish is tested on camera before it enters the collection. If it doesn't hold up under strobes, diffused daylight and tight crops, it doesn't make the cut.

III

Studio-to-set logistics

Free drop-off and pick-up across the GTA on orders over $150. Flexible rental windows for multi-day campaigns, and curated pulls delivered ready to shoot.

Rental process

From brief to backdrop in four steps.

  1. Step 01

    Browse or brief

    Explore the collection online or send over a moodboard, shot list or call-sheet. We'll suggest surfaces matched to palette, scale and lighting.

  2. Step 02

    Confirm your pull

    We build your rental kit and confirm pricing based on a one-week rental window. Longer holds available for campaigns.

  3. Step 03

    Pick-up or delivery

    Collect from our studio at 1143 Broadview, or let us drop off at your set anywhere in the GTA.

  4. Step 04

    Shoot, return, repeat

    Wrap the shoot, return the kit, and we're already here for your next brief.

About the studio

A quiet studio on Broadview, built around one obsession — the surface in the shot.

The Surface Room began with a simple observation on set: the surface makes or breaks the image. Flat, plasticky, too-uniform — and even the best food styling falls apart. So we started making our own.

Today the studio is a go-to resource for food stylists, commercial photographers and creative directors working on cookbooks, editorial features, product campaigns and restaurant launches across Canada. Every piece is built, aged and tested in-house.
